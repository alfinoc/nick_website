NA//Storefronts: Guangzhou and Shanghai

STOREFRONTS: GUANGZHOU AND SHANGHAI

Storefronts is a series of digital photographs depicting daily commercial and residential life in Shanghai and Guangzhou, taking distinctly sectioned off shops, characterized by their roll up metal grilles, as its subject matter. As a typology, the series attempts to comparatively visualize street storefronts as a stages for labor and leisure. While inherently places of business, the properties utilize every cubic meter as a venue for retail space, shipping and delivery, often extending into the sidewalk and streets. Yet, idle hours resume around economic activity. The storefront also provides a space for social interaction, rest, and mealtime, along with the spectacle of everyday affairs.

While viewed from across the street or peering into the storefront itself, the use of the street as an extension to each property is inherent in ability of the photos to observe the street and store scenes. Whether this is used to observe shopping or social interaction on the sidewalk, the series places focus on how the storefronts and those who work within them define the sidewalks, alleys and streets with a routine that uniquely combines work and pastime.
