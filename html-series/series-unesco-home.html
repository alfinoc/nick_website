UNESCO 家

A photo project concentrating on the duality of UNESCO heritage sites in Fuijian and Guangdong provinces in China as places of world heritage and places people call 家, jiā meaning home in mandarin.

Fujian Earth Houses, known as the Tulou 福建 and many built by the Hakka people 客家, are large communal courtyard complexes made from rammed earth walls, bamboo beams, wooden corridors and clay tiles. With some dating back to the 12th century, there are over 20,000 of them in contemporary Fujian province in various states of usage, decay and abandonment.

The Qing dynasty daiolou 碉樓 stand in rural Quangdong province. The watchtowers were built by rural Chinese immigrants returning from North America, interested in both displaying their wealth as well as protecting it. Through out the countryside there are just under two thousand of these towers, mostly in use by villagers or abandoned.
